Understanding Sciatica

Not every leg pain is true sciatica.

Sciatica describes symptoms caused by irritation or compression affecting the sciatic nerve. Symptoms can vary from mild discomfort to pain, tingling, numbness, or weakness that extends into one leg.

Lower Back Pain

Pain beginning in the lower back that may extend into the leg.

Leg Pain

Discomfort traveling through the buttock, thigh, calf, or foot.

Numbness & Tingling

Some patients notice tingling or altered sensation along the leg.

Mobility Changes

Walking, standing, or sitting comfortably may become more difficult.

Frequently Asked Questions

Sciatica FAQ

Can chiropractic care help with sciatica?

Many people seek chiropractic care for sciatica symptoms. After a thorough evaluation, Dr. Truong can determine whether chiropractic care may be appropriate for your condition.

How do I know if it's sciatica?

Sciatica often involves pain or unusual sensations that travel from the lower back into one leg. Because similar symptoms can have different causes, an evaluation is important.

When should I seek emergency medical care?

Seek immediate medical attention if you experience sudden loss of bowel or bladder control, severe weakness, numbness in the saddle area, or symptoms following significant trauma.
