Understanding Neck Pain

Neck discomfort can come from several sources.

Neck pain may be related to posture, long hours at a desk, phone use, stress, muscle tension, sleeping position, injury, or joint restriction. A careful evaluation helps determine whether chiropractic care may be appropriate.

Neck Stiffness

Difficulty turning your head or moving comfortably.

Shoulder Tension

Tightness across the neck, shoulders, and upper back.

Posture Strain

Discomfort related to desk work, phone use, or prolonged sitting.

Limited Mobility

Reduced range of motion that affects normal activities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Neck Pain FAQ

Can chiropractic care help with neck pain?

Many patients seek chiropractic care for neck pain, stiffness, and mobility problems. After an evaluation, Dr. Truong can determine whether chiropractic care may be appropriate for your situation.

Can posture cause neck pain?

Poor posture, prolonged desk work, and frequent phone use may contribute to neck strain and muscle tension.

When should I seek emergency care?

Seek immediate medical attention if neck pain follows major trauma or is accompanied by weakness, numbness, fever, severe headache, confusion, chest pain, or trouble walking.
