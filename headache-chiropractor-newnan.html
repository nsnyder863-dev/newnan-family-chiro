Headache & Migraine Evaluation

Headache care starts with understanding the pattern.

Headaches have many possible causes. Dr. Tuan Truong evaluates neck movement, posture, muscle tension, symptom history, and warning signs to determine whether chiropractic care may be appropriate or whether medical referral is needed.

Common Patterns

What patients often describe.

Symptoms alone do not establish the cause, but the pattern can help guide the evaluation.

01

Tension-type pressure

A tight or pressing sensation that may involve the forehead, temples, scalp, or upper neck.

02

Neck-related headache

Head pain that appears alongside neck stiffness, restricted motion, or discomfort that starts near the base of the skull.

03

Posture and screen strain

Headache symptoms that become more noticeable after prolonged desk work, driving, reading, or device use.

04

Recurring migraine history

Repeated episodes that may involve sensitivity to light or sound, nausea, or activity intolerance and may require coordinated medical care.

Your Evaluation

More than a quick neck check.

Dr. Truong begins with the timing, location, frequency, intensity, triggers, associated symptoms, prior injuries, and medical history. The examination may include posture, neck and upper-back movement, muscle tenderness, and neurologic screening when indicated.

If findings suggest that chiropractic care is not appropriate, the office may recommend medical evaluation or coordination with another healthcare professional.

Seek Immediate Care

Do not wait for a chiropractic visit when a headache has emergency warning signs.

Call 911 or seek urgent medical evaluation for a sudden severe headache, stroke-like symptoms, fainting, confusion, severe weakness or numbness, headache after significant head injury, fever with neck stiffness, or symptoms that feel distinctly different or rapidly worsen.

Care Experience

Comfort-focused and clearly explained.

When chiropractic care is appropriate, Dr. Truong explains the recommended approach before treatment begins. Care may focus on improving neck or upper-back mobility, reducing musculoskeletal tension, and addressing movement or posture factors that may be contributing to symptoms.

No specific outcome can be guaranteed. Recommendations depend on the individual evaluation, response to care, and whether other healthcare services are needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Headache care FAQ.

Can neck tension contribute to headaches?

Some headache patterns occur with neck tension, restricted movement, or discomfort near the base of the skull. An evaluation is important because headaches can have many different causes.

Do you treat migraines?

The office evaluates whether musculoskeletal factors may be contributing to a patient's symptoms. Chiropractic care is not appropriate for every migraine or headache pattern, and medical referral may be recommended.

What should I bring to the appointment?

Bring a photo ID, insurance card if applicable, medication list, and information about prior evaluations, imaging, injuries, or treatments related to the headaches.

When should I seek emergency medical care?

Seek immediate care for sudden severe headache, stroke-like symptoms, fainting, severe weakness, confusion, significant trauma, fever with neck stiffness, or any rapidly worsening or unusual presentation.
