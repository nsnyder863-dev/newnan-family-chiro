Common Causes of Back Pain

Back pain can come from many different sources. Some patients notice pain after lifting, bending, sleeping awkwardly, sitting at a desk, driving for long periods, or recovering from an injury. Others develop back pain gradually from posture, repetitive strain, muscle imbalance, or normal wear and tear.

Since the cause can vary from person to person, the first step is understanding what may be contributing to your pain and how it affects your movement.

Symptoms That May Bring Patients to a Chiropractor

Patients often visit Newnan Family Chiropractic for symptoms such as lower back pain, stiffness, soreness, tight muscles, pain after activity, discomfort while sitting or standing, and reduced range of motion.

If your back pain is severe, follows a major injury, causes weakness, numbness, bowel or bladder changes, fever, or unexplained weight loss, seek urgent medical care.

How Chiropractic Care May Help Back Pain

Chiropractic care focuses on how the spine, joints, muscles, and nervous system work together. Depending on your evaluation, care may include chiropractic adjustments, movement guidance, stretching recommendations, posture support, and practical advice to reduce stress on the back.

The goal is to help improve mobility, reduce irritation, and support better function so you can return to daily life with less discomfort.

Back Pain FAQ

When should I see a chiropractor for back pain?

Consider scheduling a visit if back pain is limiting your daily activity, keeps returning, affects your sleep, or does not improve with rest and basic self-care.

Do I need a referral?

No. In Georgia, patients do not need a referral to schedule chiropractic care.

Do you accept insurance for back pain visits?

Can chiropractic care help lower back pain?

Many patients seek chiropractic care for lower back pain, stiffness, and movement-related discomfort. Your chiropractor can evaluate your symptoms and recommend a care plan based on your needs.
