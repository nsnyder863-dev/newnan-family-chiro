Understanding Back Pain

Back pain has many possible causes.

Back pain can develop suddenly or gradually. Muscle strain, poor posture, repetitive movement, lifting injuries, arthritis, and other conditions may all contribute. A proper evaluation helps determine whether chiropractic care may be appropriate.

Lower Back Pain

Discomfort affecting the lumbar spine or surrounding muscles.

Mid Back Pain

Pain or stiffness involving the middle portion of the back.

Muscle Tightness

Restricted movement or soreness after work, exercise, or daily activities.

Reduced Mobility

Difficulty bending, twisting, standing, or walking comfortably.

Frequently Asked Questions

Back Pain FAQ

Can chiropractic care help with back pain?

Many patients seek chiropractic care for back pain. After an evaluation, Dr. Truong can determine whether chiropractic care may be appropriate for your situation.

How many visits will I need?

Treatment recommendations vary depending on your symptoms, examination findings, response to care, and personal goals.

When should I seek emergency care?

Seek immediate medical attention if you experience severe trauma, sudden loss of bowel or bladder control, progressive weakness, numbness in the saddle area, fever with severe back pain, or other emergency symptoms.
